# Compliance and Regulatory Guidelines

## Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements

All account holders must complete KYC verification before their account becomes active. KYC documentation must be refreshed every 24 months. Required documents include:
- Government-issued photo ID
- Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement dated within 90 days)
- Source of funds declaration for accounts over $500,000
- Enhanced due diligence for accounts over $2,000,000 (Diamond tier)

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

Transactions exceeding $10,000 are automatically flagged for AML review. Multiple transactions within a 24-hour period that cumulatively exceed $10,000 are also flagged (structuring detection). Suspicious activity reports (SARs) are filed with FinCEN within 30 days of detection.

### Transaction Monitoring Thresholds

| Transaction Type | Automatic Flag Threshold | Enhanced Review Threshold |
|-----------------|-------------------------|--------------------------|
| Cash deposits | $10,000 | $50,000 |
| Wire transfers (domestic) | $25,000 | $100,000 |
| Wire transfers (international) | $10,000 | $50,000 |
| Securities transfers | $50,000 | $250,000 |

## Suitability Requirements

All investment recommendations must align with the account holder's documented risk tolerance and investment goals. Advisors must document the rationale for any recommendation that deviates from the standard allocation for the client's risk profile. Clients must acknowledge and sign off on any investment that falls outside their stated risk tolerance.

### Risk Tolerance Reassessment

Risk tolerance assessments must be updated:
- At account opening
- Every 12 months during the annual review
- When there is a significant life event (marriage, divorce, retirement, inheritance)
- When the client requests a change in investment strategy
- When portfolio value crosses a tier threshold

## Data Privacy and Security

### Client Data Classification

| Data Type | Classification | Access Level |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Account balance and holdings | Confidential | Advisor + Client only |
| Personal contact information | Private | Authorized staff only |
| Transaction history | Confidential | Advisor + Client + Compliance |
| Risk assessment results | Internal | Advisor + Client + Management |
| Communication records | Internal | Advisor + Compliance |

### Data Retention Policy

- Active account records: Retained for the life of the account plus 7 years after closure
- Transaction records: 7 years from transaction date
- Communication records (emails, chat logs): 5 years
- KYC documentation: 5 years after account closure
- Compliance reports: 10 years

## Conflict of Interest Policy

Advisors must disclose any personal holdings in securities they recommend to clients. Advisors may not trade in the same securities as their clients within a 48-hour window (blackout period). Gifts from clients exceeding $100 in value must be reported to compliance.

## Complaint Handling Procedure

1. All client complaints must be acknowledged within 24 hours
2. Written complaints must receive a formal response within 15 business days
3. Complaints involving potential financial harm are escalated to the compliance officer immediately
4. Resolution timeline: 30 business days for standard complaints, 60 business days for complex cases
5. All complaints and resolutions are logged in the compliance management system

## Regulatory Reporting

The firm files the following regulatory reports:
- Form ADV: Updated annually and within 90 days of any material change
- Form PF: Quarterly for accounts with AUM exceeding $150 million
- FINRA BrokerCheck: Updated within 30 days of any reportable event
- SEC 13F: Quarterly disclosure of holdings exceeding $100 million
